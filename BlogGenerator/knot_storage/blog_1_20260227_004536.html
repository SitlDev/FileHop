RFK Jr.'s CDC panel to discuss Covid vaccine injuries in upcoming meeting

Atlas Blackwood · February 27, 2026 · 5 min read

**Vaccine Safety in the Spotlight: RFK Jr.'s CDC Panel to Discuss Covid-19 Vaccine Injuries**

In a move that is sending shockwaves throughout the medical community, Robert F. Kennedy Jr., a prominent vaccine safety advocate, has announced plans to convene a panel at the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) to discuss alleged Covid-19 vaccine injuries.

As the world continues to grapple with the ongoing pandemic, concerns over the safety and efficacy of vaccines have reached a fever pitch. With more than 6 billion doses of Covid-19 vaccines administered worldwide, reports of adverse reactions and injuries are on the rise.

Kennedy, a longtime advocate for vaccine safety and founder of the World Mercury Project, has been vocal about his concerns regarding the potential risks associated with vaccination. His upcoming panel at the CDC is expected to bring together experts from various fields to share their findings and insights on the topic.

"The public has a right to know the truth about vaccine safety," Kennedy said in a statement. "As we continue to roll out these vaccines, it's essential that we take a closer look at the potential risks and consequences. I'm thrilled to have this opportunity to bring together some of the brightest minds in the field to explore this critical issue."

The panel is expected to cover a range of topics, including the scientific basis for vaccine injuries, case studies of alleged adverse reactions, and an examination of the CDC's own data on vaccine safety.
