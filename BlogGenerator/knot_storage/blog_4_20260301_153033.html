Hizballah's origins date back to the 1980s, when it emerged as a response to Israel's occupation of Lebanon. The group's initial goal was to drive Israeli forces out of Lebanese territory and promote Shia interests in the region. Over time, Hizballah has evolved into a powerful political force within Lebanon, with its leader Hassan Nasrallah holding significant sway over the country's government.
