**Vaccine Injuries Take Center Stage as RFK Jr.'s CDC Panel Meets**

In a move that promises to spark intense debate and scrutiny, Robert F. Kennedy Jr.'s Children's Health Defense (CHD) has announced an upcoming meeting of the CDC's Advisory Committee on Immunization Practices (ACIP) to discuss Covid vaccine injuries.

The meeting, set to take place on [Date], marks a significant shift in the national conversation around vaccination, as it brings together experts from both sides of the immunization debate. The ACIP, which is responsible for advising the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) on vaccine policy, has historically focused on promoting vaccination rates rather than examining potential harm.

According to CHD, the meeting will feature testimony from families who have experienced adverse reactions to Covid vaccines, as well as presentations from experts in the fields of medicine, epidemiology, and toxicology. [PHOTO1: A group of families affected by vaccine injuries gather outside the CDC headquarters]

Using data from the Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS), CHD estimates that tens of thousands of individuals have reported experiencing adverse reactions to Covid vaccines, including life-threatening conditions such as anaphylaxis and blood clotting disorders. Critics argue that these injuries are not adequately addressed by the current vaccine safety monitoring system.

"This meeting is a critical step towards acknowledging the reality of vaccine injuries and holding our public health agencies accountable for their actions," said RFK Jr., CHD's chairman and chief strategist, in a statement. "We will be presenting evidence that challenges the status quo on vaccine safety and encourages a more transparent approach to addressing adverse reactions."
