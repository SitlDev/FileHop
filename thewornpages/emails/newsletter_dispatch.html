Entry No. 014 • November 2024

The Whispering Library

As the autumn wind begins to settle into the eaves of our archival cottage, the scent of parched linen and old mahogany becomes even more pronounced. This month, we have been exploring the theme of 'Intimate Gothic'—stories that don't just tell of hauntings, but of the way memories adhere to the foundations of a home.

Our scouts have returned from a particularly bountiful estate sale in the Cotswolds with a collection of 19th-century ghost stories, each bound in deep forest-green cloth. They feel as though they were meant to be read by the light of a single, flickering candle.

This Month’s Curation Spotlight

We are particularly enamored with a first-edition translation of *The Shadow Guest*. Its previous owner, a librarian from Oxford, left a small, pressed fern between the final two pages—a silent testament to a moment of quiet reflection over a century ago.

Members of the **Collector's Vault** can view the high-resolution digital scans of these new acquisitions starting tomorrow morning at 08:00 BST.

Until next we meet among the stacks,

The Curator
