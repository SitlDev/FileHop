Creating Portmanteau Words

A portmanteau is a new word created by blending two existing words together. This is a creative linguistic technique used in marketing, product naming, and modern slang. Examples abound in contemporary language!

Famous Portmanteau Examples:

  • BREAKFAST + LUNCH = BRUNCH - meal eaten in late morning
  • MOTOR + HOTEL = MOTEL - roadside lodging for travelers
  • EMOTION + ICON = EMOJI - small digital image expressing feeling
  • INTERNET + SECURITY = INTERNETSECURITY - digital protection
  • SMOKE + FOG = SMOG - air pollution from smoke and fog
  • FLUTTER + TWITTER = FLUTTER - social media metaphor

Historical Origins:

The term "portmanteau" was coined by Lewis Carroll in "Through the Looking-Glass" (1871) to describe words like "slithy" (slimy + lithe). However, the practice of blending words is ancient—Latin, Greek, and Old English all contain examples. Modern portmanteaus exploded with brand naming in the late 20th century (MOTEL, BRUNCH, SMOG) and became ubiquitous in digital age (SELFIE, CRYPTOCURRENCY, INFODEMIC).
