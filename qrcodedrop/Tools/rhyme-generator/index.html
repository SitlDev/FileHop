The Art of Rhyming

Rhyming words are essential for poetry, songwriting, and creative writing. A rhyme occurs when two or more words have similar ending sounds. Our rhyme generator instantly finds perfect and near-perfect rhymes for any word.

Types of Rhymes:

  • Perfect Rhymes (True Rhymes): Same vowel and ending consonant sounds (cat/bat, moon/June)
  • Near Rhymes (Slant/Imperfect): Similar but not identical sounds (home/come, reason/prison)
  • Internal Rhymes: Rhymes within a single line ("Once upon a time so fine")
  • Eye Rhymes: Look similar but don't sound the same (cough/through)

Example: Words That Rhyme with "LOVE"

Common rhymes and near-rhymes for the word "love":

  • DOVE - a peaceful bird, or past tense of dive
  • ABOVE - higher than something
  • GLOVE - hand protection worn for warmth
  • SHOVE - to push roughly
  • THEREOF - of that thing (archaic)

Historical Evolution of Rhyming:

Rhyming emerged as a literary device in early Arabic poetry around the 5th century and became central to European poetry by the Medieval period. The earliest rhyme schemes appeared in Old French (11th century), while Old English poetry relied on alliteration instead. Rhyme became standardized in Shakespeare's sonnets and Milton's Paradise Lost. Digital rhyme generators emerged in the 1990s with the advent of online poetry tools, revolutionizing songwriting and creative composition.
