Planning for Life's Major Milestones

The Long-Term Logic of College Savings

Education is often the single largest investment a family makes outside of a home. The challenge is the "time horizon"—the years between a child's birth and their first tuition bill. Our College Goal Tool helps you work backward from your desired goal, factoring in your current savings and expected market returns. By starting early, you allow Compound Interest to do the heavy lifting. Even small monthly contributions made over 18 years can result in a significant "interest gift" from the market, reducing the amount you need to save out-of-pocket.

When planning for college, it's also vital to consider the "Inflation of Education." Traditionally, college tuition has increased at a faster rate than the general Consumer Price Index (CPI). If your goal is $100,000 in today's dollars, you may actually need $200,000 by the time your child reaches 18. Consistently reviewing your savings progress and adjusting for these shifts ensures that you stay on track to meet your educational objectives without taking on excessive student loan debt.

Wedding Budgeting: Managing the "Big Day"

Weddings are emotionally charged events, which often leads to "budget creep." Our Wedding Budget Splitter provides a realistic framework for distributing your total budget. Traditionally, the venue and catering account for 40-50% of total costs. By setting these boundaries early, you can avoid the stress of overspending on minor details at the expense of the core experience. We also include a mandatory 10% Emergency Buffer—because in the world of weddings, there is always an unexpected expense.

The key to a successful wedding budget is prioritization. Decide on the three most important elements (e.g., photography, food, music) and allocate your resources there. By being disciplined about the "nice-to-haves" versus the "must-haves," you can create a memorable celebration that doesn't leave you with a financial hangover. Remember: a wedding is a day, but a marriage is a lifetime.

The Math of Time and Age

Time is our only truly non-renewable resource. Our Duration & Age Tools help you visualize time in more granular ways. Whether you are tracking the days between two life events or calculating exactly how long you've been on this planet, these metrics provide perspective. For many, seeing a deadline in "days" rather than "months" creates a sense of urgency that can improve productivity and decision-making. Use these tools to audit your time and ensure you are spending it on the things that truly matter.
